# AI adoption opportunity discovery methods (quick guide)

This is a practical shortlist of methods for finding good AI adoption opportunities before design/build starts.

## 1) workflow mapping
Map the workflow as it is actually performed: steps, owners, handoffs, tools, delays, and failure points.

**Use when:** teams describe work differently and there is no shared process picture.

## 2) discovery sessions with team leaders
Structured workshops with process owners to surface painpoints, bottlenecks, repetitive work, and known risk points.

**Use when:** you need fast initial candidate generation and stakeholder buy-in.

## 3) employee surveys
Short surveys to capture pain frequency, friction severity, and perceived automation suitability across roles.

**Use when:** you need broad signal quickly across many teams.

## 4) workplace shadowing
Observe real work execution to identify hidden workarounds, tool switching, and rework that people often forget to mention.

**Use when:** stated process and actual process do not match.

## 5) time-spend analysis
Quantify where time goes (repetitive admin vs judgement work vs waiting/handoffs) over 1-2 weeks.

**Use when:** you need measurable baseline and impact potential.

## 6) process mining (event-log analysis)
Use system logs to reconstruct real process flows, bottlenecks, conformance issues, and cycle times.

**Use when:** reliable event data exists in CRM/ERP/service systems.

## 7) retrospectives and incident review
Review post-mortems and recurring incidents to identify workflows where quality and consistency repeatedly fail.

**Use when:** teams already have retrospective artifacts and incident logs.

## 8) tool stack and data-flow audit
Inventory tools and data movement to detect overlap, manual copy/paste paths, integration gaps, and governance risk.

**Use when:** tool sprawl and duplicate systems are suspected.

## 9) champion network method
Use a small cross-functional champion group to surface candidate workflows, challenge assumptions, and validate pilotability.

**Use when:** adoption risk is high and local team trust is critical.

## 10) value-effort-risk scoring workshop
Score candidate workflows against business value, implementation effort, boundary clarity, and risk exposure.

**Use when:** you need to prioritise a shortlist into a first pilot.

---

## practical output after discovery
A ranked shortlist of 3-5 candidate workflows, each with:
- discovery evidence
- baseline metrics
- why AI (vs non-AI change)
- boundary notes (policy + capability + approval gates)
- pilotability assessment
